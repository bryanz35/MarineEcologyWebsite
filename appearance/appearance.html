The Oceanic Giant Manta Ray

Anatomy & Appearance

The Giant Manta Ray is the largest species of ray with a wingspan of up to 29 feet. It has a large, diamond-shaped body with wing-like pectoral fins, gill slits along its center, horn-shaped cephalic fins, and a large mouth. It's very wide but thin, similar to a blanket. Like many other fish, the Giant Manta Ray is dark on the top (dorsal) side and light on the bottom (ventral) side. Manta rays have distinct spot patterns on their bellies that can be used as identification.

Locomotion

The Giant Manta Ray swims steadily while traveling in deep water but idly closer to shore. This allows it to conserve energy in the open sea but filter feed effectively in shallow water. The Giant Manta Ray needs to swim continuously in order to breathe.

The Giant Manta Ray swims by flapping its pectoral fins, which are connected to its body by flexible cartilage. It can also use its cephalic fins to steer and maneuver. The Giant Manta Ray can swim at speeds of up to 24 km/h (15 mph).
