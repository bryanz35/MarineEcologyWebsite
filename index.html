Introduction

The Giant Oceanic Manta Ray (Mobula birostris), also known as the Giant Manta Ray, is the largest species of manta ray. Its name is derived from the Spanish word for blanket or cloak, "Manta", and it's also known as the devilfish because of its horn-shaped cephalic fins. Baby rays are called pups, and groups of rays are called squadrons.

Classification

The Giant Oceanic Manta Ray is a member of the Mobulidae family. It belongs to the phylum Chordata, class Choindrichtyes, order Rajiformes, family Mobulidae, and genus Mobula. Reef manta rays(M. alfredi) and spinetail mobulas (M. japanica) are also related to the giant oceanic manta ray.

A relatively recent update to the taxonomic arrangement of the Manta family has now combined genus Manta with genus Mobula, so the Giant Manta Ray is often referred to as Manta birostris instead of Mobula birostris.

Fun Facts

Giant manta rays are the biggest rays in the world. Additionally, they can dive more than 1,000 meters underwater and have the largest brain volumes of any fish. When feeding, Giant Manta Rays will do somersaults when feeding on plankton, and consume up to 60 pounds of plankton per day!
